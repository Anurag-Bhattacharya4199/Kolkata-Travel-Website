Main Social Events Some Greatest from Kolkata

Main Social Events

Kolkata has a cosmopolitan culture where people from various parts of the world have immigrated over the history of periods and decided to make the city their home. People of Kolkata celebrate each other's festivals irrespective of their religious beliefs. There is a local saying that Kolkata people celebrate 13 festivals in 12 months of the year which starts with the English New Year in January and ends with the Christmas celebration in December. Every season of the year has its special festivals.

Durga Puja

Durga Puja in Kolkata is an annual festival celebrated in September or October. It marks the worship of the Hindu mother goddess Durga. This festival is the biggest religious festival of Bengalis in Kolkata, however, it is celebrated by every resident in the city irrespective of religion making it one of the biggest festivals in India. The Durga Puja of Kolkata is unique and recently awarded with UNESCO Heritage Festival attracting an enormous number of people across the world to visit Kolkata during the festive time.

Christmas Celebration in Park Street

Every December, The Kolkata Christmas Festival lights up the bustling Park Street. Themed decorations take to the streets while twinkling LED ornaments sparkle after sundown. The streets chime in harmonious carols, while a beautiful Christmas tree decked in baubles awaits at the end of Allen Park.

Eid

Similarly, Eid is celebrated all over Kolkata. Special Eid Prayer is held at Red Road, at the heart of Kolkata and in different Mosques and areas across the city. People across the City irrespective of their religions celebrate the festive with greetings, gatherings, and good food.
