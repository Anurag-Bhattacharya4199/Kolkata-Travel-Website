Visit Kolkata!

Kolkata, the ‘CITY OF JOY’ is an important place in the economy, politics, and culture of Modern India. Located in eastern India, the 300+ years old city has colonial architecture and has maintained its quintessential charm through different periods of history. Kolkata is famous for its rich culture, tradition, literature, and food, and it is an immensely popular tourist destination.

Shantiniketan

Shantiniketan is a beautiful place near Bolpur and about 212 km from Kolkata in the North Central region of West Bengal in the Birbhum district. “Shantiniketan” means “the abode of peace” originally founded by Maharshi Devendranath Tagore, a renowned preacher of the Brahma religion and a great philosopher. Originally, he decided to create an Ashram for meditation in the serene red soil region surrounded by Chhatim trees. Later, his famous son, the world-famous Noble Laureate poet, author, dramatist, composer and artist, Gurudev Rabindranath Tagore opened the first open-air school in 1905. By 1921, the school was expanded to Viswa Bharathi University famous for its studies in Arts.
Shantiniketan located in the region of rustic red soil, verdant woods and serenity that pulsates in the air has unique calm nature and is a perfect place to escape from the busy city of Kolkata. The town of Bolpur forms itself with Shantiniketan, a famous university for Arts and Agriculture and Srineketan, famous for handicraft work to explore. One can reach Bolpur through 2-3 hours of train ride from Kolkata or by road.
Shantiniketan is a place of fairs and festivals. It starts with Nababarsha, the Bengali new year in mid-April, followed by Rabindra Janmotsab, the birth anniversary of Gurudev Rabindranath Tagore in May. Brikhropon, the festival of planting saplings and Varshamangal, the monsoon festival, are celebrated in August. Poush mela is organized in winter, in December to mark the harvest season with hundreds of stalls and people from all over the world visiting it during this time. The Vasanta Utsab or the festival of colour is celebrated in March for the onset of spring.

Darjeeling

Darjeeling is an international tourist destination in northern West Bengal and is about 650 km away from Kolkata. The popular Hill city of Darjeeling is at 6710 ft altitude and located in the foothills of the Himalayan range with a spectacular view of the Singalila range and Mt. Kanchandzonga bounded by the state of Sikkim in the North, Bhutan in its East, and Nepal in its West. It is a beautiful hill station with a view of snow-filled mountain ranges, river streams flowing down mountain slopes, lovely tea gardens and a landscape with firs, pines, and ferns with friendly people and modern yet colonial charm.

Weather in Darjeeling is unique in each season with pleasant summer in May-June, with little chill and clear sky, monsoon in July to September, lots of rain, and fog, cloudy Autumn from October to November, and chilly winter from December to January and clear sky Spring in March to April.

People from neighbouring states and even countries immigrated to Darjeeling. The majority of people are Nepalese along with Tibetans, Lepchas, and Bengalis. Darjeeling is famous for its Tea, Nepali food, snack items like Momos, and Tibetan items like Thakpu.
