Special Foods of Kolkata

Kolkata is famous for its Street Food as well as Fish, Authentic Bengali, and Mughlai (Mughal) dishes.

Here are glimpses:

Kolkata Street Foods

Kolkata Street Food is extremely popular for mouth-watering snack items Phuchka, Egg Rolls, Noodles (Chow mien), Kathi Rolls, Luchi and Alu Dum (special potato curry), Ghugni, Mughlai Paratha, Singara. Street food and tea stalls are located on every street corner in the city.

Sweets

Bengalis are fond of Sweets and some of the famous Sweets are Rossogolla, Pantuwa, Langcha, and Sandesh.

Some of the famous Sweet Shops are GANGURAM & SONS, SEN MAHASAY, BALARAM MULLICK & RADHARAMAN MULLICK, GIRISH CHANDRA DEY & NAKUR CHANDRA NANDY, KC DAS.

Fish Dishes

Kolkata is the land of Fish and exotic Fish Dishes. Some of the famous fishes are Ilish, a fish which is ONLY available fresh in rivers of West Bengal in India Bangladesh. There are many other famous fishes in the region like Chingri (large freshwater Prawn) and various other fishes.

Some of the specialty restaurants for exotic Bengali (locals of Kolkata) Dishes are Oh! Calcutta (Old British name of Kolkata), KOPAI (Heritage Bengali Cuisine), Bhojohari Manna, 6 Ballygunge Place, Aheli (Peerless Hotel).

Kolkata Biryani

Calcutta or Kolkata Biryani evolved from the Lucknow style when Awadh's last Nawab Wajid Ali Shah was exiled in 1856 to the Kolkata suburb of Metiabruz. Shah brought his chef with him. The Kolkata biriyani is characterized by the unique presence of potato in it, along with meat and egg ('dim' in Bengali).
